The Issue

In China, the concentration of political power within the Chinese Communist Party has raised ongoing concerns about human rights, freedom of speech, and the abuse of justice systems to maintain control. Surveillance, censorship, and detention of political dissidents remain key challenges.

Did you know? China has over 200 million surveillance cameras installed nationwide, making it the country with the most extensive surveillance network in the world.

Latest News About China

    Impact

    Citizens are affected by mass surveillance, lack of free press, internet censorship, arbitrary detentions, and restricted access to justice. Minority groups such as Uyghurs have been subjected to re-education camps, while journalists and activists are often silenced. These issues violate several articles of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights, which serves as an international standard for the protection of human dignity.

    Internet Censorship

    China's "Great Firewall" blocks access to numerous foreign websites and services, including Google, Facebook, Twitter, and many news outlets. The government employs over 2 million people to monitor online content and remove material deemed sensitive or harmful to state interests.

    Freedom of Press

    China ranks 177th out of 180 countries in the Press Freedom Index. Journalists who report on sensitive topics often face harassment, job loss, imprisonment, or forced confessions. Foreign correspondents frequently experience obstacles and intimidation.

    Religious Persecution

    Religious groups outside state-sanctioned organizations face severe restrictions. This includes Tibetan Buddhists, Christians in underground churches, Falun Gong practitioners, and Muslims, particularly in Xinjiang where over a million Uyghurs have reportedly been detained in "re-education" facilities.

    Quick Facts

    Surveillance Technology

    China has an estimated 200+ million surveillance cameras—that's approximately one camera for every seven citizens. Many of these cameras are equipped with facial recognition technology that can identify individuals in real-time and track their movements across cities.

    Internet Censorship

    China employs over 2 million internet censors to monitor and control online content. The "Great Firewall" blocks access to over 10,000 domain names including Google, Facebook, Twitter, and many international news outlets. VPN use is restricted, and violations can result in fines or imprisonment.

    Social Credit System

    China's social credit system rates citizens based on behaviors ranging from financial creditworthiness to social behaviors. Low scores can result in restrictions on travel, access to education, slower internet speeds, and public shaming. The system is expected to be fully operational nationwide by 2025.
